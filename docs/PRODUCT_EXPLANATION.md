# Product Explanation

## Short Version

Chef OS is a mobile-first operating system for professional kitchens.

It helps chefs, sous-chefs, and cooks run a shift without chaos: everyone sees their station, personal instructions, recipes, checklists, stock signals, stop-list, and the overall kitchen picture in one place.

## Who It Is For

- Chef: sees the whole shift, stop-list, staff, station progress, stock signals, and activity history.
- Sous-chef: controls execution, confirms problems, follows up on stock and station blockers.
- Cook: sees exactly what to do on their station, follows process checklists, opens recipe instructions, and signals problems quickly.
- Purchaser/admin: later receives confirmed stock signals and turns them into supplier orders.

## The Problem

Professional kitchens work fast. During service, people do not have time to search through chats, paper checklists, spreadsheets, recipe folders, and verbal instructions.

Common problems:

- cooks do not know the current stop-list;
- station setup is inconsistent;
- stock problems are reported too late;
- recipes and process rules live in different places;
- managers cannot see which checklists are actually done;
- new staff need simple station instructions.

## What We Are Building

Chef OS combines:

- shift command screen;
- overall checklist for the whole kitchen;
- process checklists for every station;
- cook profile and personal instruction;
- common instructions for everyone;
- station process guides;
- recipe/TTK cards;
- inventory signals;
- staff calls;
- chat;
- activity history.

The quick action button is designed for service pressure. It does not ask a cook to fill out a form. It asks "What happened?" and lets them signal things like product is empty, one unit is left, the ticket is delayed, a station is blocked, or the sous-chef is needed.

## Why It Is Different

Many tools focus on one part of the work:

- checklists only;
- recipes and food cost only;
- inventory only;
- staff scheduling only.

Chef OS is designed around the real kitchen shift. It connects station work, chef control, stock signals, process instructions, and checklists into one simple mobile workflow.

## How To Explain It To A Restaurant Operator

"This is an app for the kitchen shift. The chef sets the station processes, checklists, recipes, and stop-list. Every cook opens the app and sees their station, their personal instruction, what needs to be done, and quick buttons to report problems. The sous-chef sees the whole picture: which stations are ready, what stock is low, who is working, and what has been completed."
