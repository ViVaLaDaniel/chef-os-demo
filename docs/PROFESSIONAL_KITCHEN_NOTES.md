# Professional Kitchen Notes

These notes explain why Chef OS is structured around stations, mise en place, fast signals, and safety reminders.

## Sources Reviewed

- FDA Food Code: https://www.fda.gov/food/fda-food-code/food-code-2022
- FDA Employee Health and Personal Hygiene: https://www.fda.gov/food/retail-food-industryregulatory-assistance-training/retail-food-protection-employee-health-and-personal-hygiene-handbook
- Michelin guide note on mise en place: https://guide.michelin.com/us/en/article/features/mise-en-place-cooking
- Professional mise en place/station organization reference: https://mise-en-place.recipes/philosophy/professional-kitchen

## Product Implications

- Kitchen work is station-based, so the app must show station responsibility and station process first.
- Mise en place is a pre-service operating state, not just a recipe concept. Tasks should be tied to shift, station, deadline, and responsible person.
- During service, staff need one-tap signals instead of long forms.
- Food safety reminders should stay close to the station instruction: handwashing, allergens, temperature awareness, clean tools, labeling, and cross-contamination control.
- The pass/sous-chef should confirm operational truth. Cooks should signal problems quickly, not mutate final stock or stop-list authority without review.

## UX Constraints

- All controls must be reachable by thumb.
- Critical states must be visually obvious.
- Station instruction should be available without search.
- Bottom navigation must never hide the active work item.
